US Healthcare Analysis Dashboard

Power BI dashboard analyzing patient admissions, diagnostic outcomes, healthcare utilization, and healthcare costs using a 55,000+ record healthcare dataset.

📌 Project Overview

This project explores patient admissions, diagnostic patterns, healthcare utilization, and financial trends within a healthcare dataset. The objective was to transform raw healthcare data into an interactive Power BI dashboard that supports operational reporting and healthcare decision-making.

The final solution includes four interactive dashboard pages focused on admissions, diagnosis trends, cost and utilization analysis, and patient-level exploration.

🎯 Business Challenge

Healthcare organizations need visibility into patient populations, admission patterns, diagnostic outcomes, treatment costs, and resource utilization.

The goal was to build an interactive reporting solution that helps stakeholders identify trends and better understand healthcare operations.

🛠️ Process

  • Imported healthcare data into Power BI.
  • Cleaned and transformed the dataset using Power Query.
  • Created Patient ID, Admission Year, Age Group, Length of Stay Categories, and Billing Categories.
  • Built DAX measures for KPI reporting and trend analysis.
  • Designed a four-page dashboard focused on admissions, diagnostics, utilization, and patient exploration.
  • Added slicers, conditional formatting, and interactive filtering capabilities.

📊 Dashboard Page 1 — Admission Overview

Key Takeaways

  • Over 55,000 patient records were analyzed.
  • Admissions remained relatively consistent throughout the year.
  • Long-stay patients represented the largest utilization category.
  • Patient populations were relatively balanced across major conditions.

🩺 Dashboard Page 2 — Diagnosis Overview

Key Takeaways

  • Test result distributions varied across medical conditions.
  • Medication usage patterns differed by diagnosis.
  • Age groups showed subtle differences in outcomes.
  • Clinical patterns became easier to explore through interactive visuals.

💰 Dashboard Page 3 — Cost & Utilization

Key Takeaways

  • Most patients fell into moderate and high-cost categories.
  • Length of stay varied slightly across admission types.
  • Average billing remained relatively consistent across providers.
  • Utilization metrics provided stronger insights than patient volume alone.

🔍 Dashboard Page 4 — Patient Explorer

Key Takeaways

  • Dynamic slicers support self-service analysis.
  • Patient-level detail enables deeper exploration.
  • Conditional formatting highlights important information.
  • Users can filter records across multiple healthcare dimensions.

🧪 Data Quality & Limitations

This project used a synthetic healthcare dataset rather than real patient records. While the data resembles realistic healthcare information, it does not represent actual patients or healthcare organizations.

Many variables were intentionally balanced, resulting in fewer dramatic differences between patient groups than would typically be found in real-world healthcare data. Because of this, the analysis focused more heavily on healthcare utilization, treatment costs, admission patterns, and operational trends.

The dataset also represents a snapshot in time and does not include complete patient histories or post-discharge outcomes.

💡 Business Recommendations

  • Monitor healthcare utilization trends regularly.
  • Continue tracking treatment cost distributions.
  • Expand future analyses with additional clinical variables.
  • Leverage interactive dashboards to support operational planning.
  • Use data-driven reporting to improve visibility into healthcare performance.
