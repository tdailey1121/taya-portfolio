Credit Card Customer Retention & Churn Analytics Dashboard

Power BI dashboard analyzing customer attrition, usage behavior, credit activity, and retention opportunities.

📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes credit card customer retention for PremierBank. The dashboard explores which customers are more likely to leave, what behaviors may signal churn, and which customer segments may need targeted retention strategies.

🎯 Business Challenge

PremierBank is experiencing customer attrition and inconsistent credit card usage across its customer base. Leadership needs to better understand which customer attributes and behaviors are associated with churn.

The goal was to build an interactive Power BI dashboard that identifies retention patterns, highlights at-risk segments, and supports operational recommendations for improving customer engagement.

🛠️ Process

  • Imported the credit card customer dataset into Power BI.
  • Used Power Query to clean and standardize inconsistent category labels.
  • Recoded customer attrition values into Active and Attrited categories.
  • Created grouped fields including Age Group, Tenure Group, Inactivity Band, Contact Frequency, and Utilization Band.
  • Developed DAX measures for retention rate, attrition rate, average utilization, transaction behavior, and customer risk.
  • Designed a three-page dashboard focused on segmentation, churn drivers, and retention optimization.

📊 Dashboard Page 1 — Customer Insights & Segmentation

The first page focuses on identifying which customer attributes are associated with retention and attrition. It includes demographic and product-level views across age, gender, income, education, and card category.

Customer Insights & Segmentation — attrition by demographics and card category.

Key Takeaways

  • The overall customer retention rate is approximately 84%.
  • Attrition varies across age groups, income categories, education levels, and card categories.
  • Platinum card customers show the highest attrition rate compared to other card categories.
  • This page helps identify which customer groups may need additional retention focus.

🔍 Dashboard Page 2 — Customer Behavior & Churn Drivers

The second page focuses on customer behaviors that may signal churn, including inactivity, transaction activity, utilization, tenure, and contact frequency.

Customer Behavior & Churn Drivers — transaction activity, utilization, inactivity, and contact frequency.

Key Takeaways

  • Customers with higher contact frequency experience higher attrition rates.
  • Customers with higher inactivity levels generally complete fewer transactions.
  • Transaction activity and utilization patterns vary across tenure groups.
  • Behavioral metrics can help identify early warning signs of churn.

🎯 Dashboard Page 3 — Retention & Risk Optimization

The final page translates customer behavior into retention opportunities. It highlights high-risk customers, utilization patterns, available credit, product engagement, and card category performance.

Retention & Risk Optimization — risk indicators and retention opportunities.

Key Takeaways

  • Approximately 737 customers were identified as high risk.
  • Customers with no card utilization show the highest attrition risk.
  • Available credit varies across card categories.
  • Retention efforts should focus on inactive customers, low-utilization customers, and higher-risk card segments.

🧪 Data Quality & Assumptions

Issues Identified

  • Inconsistent education labels: Some values in the Education Level field contained spelling or formatting issues and were standardized during cleaning.
  • Attrition recoding: The Attrition_Flag field was recoded into Active and Attrited categories to make dashboard interpretation easier.
  • Grouped fields: Age Group, Tenure Group, Inactivity Band, Contact Frequency, and Utilization Band were created to support clearer trend analysis.
  • Single-point-in-time data: The dataset does not include full customer history over time, so the analysis focuses on patterns and indicators rather than exact causes of attrition.

Modeling Assumptions

  • Attrition_Flag was used as the main indicator of customer churn.
  • Customers labeled as Active were treated as retained customers.
  • Customers labeled as Attrited were treated as closed or lost accounts.
  • Behavioral metrics such as inactivity, contact frequency, transaction count, and utilization were treated as churn indicators.

💡 Business Recommendations

  • Increase outreach efforts for inactive customers before they fully disengage.
  • Monitor customers with high contact frequency to identify possible service issues or dissatisfaction.
  • Create targeted retention campaigns for Platinum card customers and other high-risk card segments.
  • Encourage card usage among customers with low or no utilization through personalized offers or engagement campaigns.
  • Continue tracking churn indicators regularly to identify customer risk earlier.
